**Authors:** Ziyi Ding, Xiao-Ping Zhang

**TL;DR:** We study bivariate causal direction identification in linear acyclic models with independent noise and propose **GaussDetect-LiNGAM**, an independence-only decision pipeline. Instead of explicitly testing Gaussianity, we use directional residual-independence tests with an abstention branch. The key theoretical fact: under standard non-degeneracy conditions, *reverse residual independence implies Gaussian disturbance in the opposite direction*, supporting diagnosis of identifiable vs. non-identifiable regimes without a separate Gaussianity test. Empirically, the method yields stable test-decision patterns while reducing tests per decision (TPD) compared with a Gaussianity-first pipeline across noise families and sample sizes.
